If given, the data subject has the right to obtain confirmation from the controller as to whether or not their personal data is being processed. The data subject also has the right to access their personal data and receive information about the purposes of the processing, the categories of personal data involved, and the recipients or categories of recipients to whom the data has been or will be disclosed. If possible, the controller should also provide information about the expected storage period or the criteria used to determine it. The data subject also has the right to request rectification, erasure, or restriction of processing of their personal data, as well as the right to object to such processing. They also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority and receive information about the source of their personal data if it was not collected from them. Additionally, the data subject has the right to be informed about any automated decision-making processes involving their personal data and the potential consequences of such processing. If personal data is transferred to a third country or international organization, the data subject must be informed about the appropriate safeguards in place. The controller must provide a copy of the personal data being processed, and may charge a reasonable fee for additional copies. If the data subject requests the information electronically, it must be provided in a commonly used electronic form. This right to obtain a copy does not negatively impact the rights and freedoms of others.
