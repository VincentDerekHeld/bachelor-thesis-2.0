A small company manufactures customized bicycles.
 The sales department receives an order. A new process instance is created.
 A member of the sales department has the choice to reject or accept the order for a customized bike.
 In the former case, the process instance finishes.
 The storehouse is informed and the engineering department is also informed.
 The storehouse processes the part list of the order immediately. The storehouse checks the required quantity of each part.
 "If the part is available in-house, they reserve it."
 If it is not available, they back-order it.
 They repeat this procedure for each item on the part list.
 The engineering department prepares everything for the assembling of the ordered bicycle. Next, the assembling of the ordered bicycle takes place.
 The storehouse reserves or back-orders every item of the part list. The preparation activity finishes. The engineering department assembles the bicycle.
 The sales department ships the bicycle to the customer. The sales department finishes the process instance.
