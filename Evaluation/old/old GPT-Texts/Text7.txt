Top management must review the organization's information security management system at planned intervals.The management reviews the status of actions from previous reviews, considers changes in external and internal issues relevant to the information security management system, considers changes in needs and expectations of interested parties relevant to the information security management system, considers feedback on the information security performance, including trends in nonconformities and corrective actions, monitoring and measurement results, audit results, and fulfilment of information security objectives, considers feedback from interested parties, considers results of risk assessment and status of risk treatment plan, and considers opportunities for continual improvement. They must then decide related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to the information security management system.The organization must determine the methods for monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation.Management must make documented information available as evidence of their results.
