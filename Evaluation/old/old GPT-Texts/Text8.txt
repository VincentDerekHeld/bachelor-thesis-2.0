The Controller must notify the Supervisory Authority competent in accordance with Article 55 of the personal data breach,
unless the personal data breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
The organization must notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours.
If the notification is not made within 72 hours, the organization must provide reasons for the delay.
The processor must notify the controller without undue delay.
Unauthorized access of he to confidential information must be prevented.
The organization shall communicate the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point.
Organization must determine the likely consequences of the personal data breach, which could include identity theft, financial loss, damage to reputation, and potential legal action.
The Controller must describe the measures taken or proposed to be taken to address the personal data breach in the notification.
The organization must provide the information in phases.Organization must determine methods for monitoring, measurement, analysis, and evaluation.
Organization must document any personal data breaches they have experienced.
The Supervisory Authority must be able to verify compliance by documenting.
