The data subject has the right to obtain erasure of personal data concerning him or her from the controller without undue delay.
The controller must erase personal data without undue delay when one of the specified grounds applies.
The personal data is no longer necessary for the purposes they were collected for.
The data subject withdraws consent, and there's no other legal ground for processing.
If the data subject objects to processing and there are no overriding legitimate grounds, the data must be erased.
The personal data has been unlawfully processed.
The personal data must be erased to comply with a legal obligation in Union or Member State law.
The personal data was collected in relation to the offer of information society services.
If the controller has made personal data public and must erase it, the controller must inform other controllers processing the data about the data subject's erasure request.
Processing is not necessary if it infringes on the right of freedom of expression and information.
Processing isn't required if it's for compliance with a legal obligation or for performing a task in the public interest.
Processing isn't necessary if it's for public interest in public health.
Processing isn't needed if it's for archiving in public interest, research, or statistical purposes and if erasure would impair those purposes.
Processing isn't necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.
